Getting Started

Welcome to a Different Kind of Health Archive

If you were to send this project to your auntie, grandma, or someone who doesn't spend their days thinking about digital humanities, what would they need to know?

This archive is about real people's health experiences, not perfect wellness advice. Here you'll find authentic stories from Bay Area residents about how they actually take care of their health‚Äîincluding the challenges, adaptations, and community support that wellness magazines often ignore.

Quick Start (3 minutes)

  1. Start with the Mini-Exhibit

    Get the big picture of what this archive is about and why it matters

  2. Browse a few Archive Stories

    Click on story titles that interest you‚Äîaudio, photos, videos, and written reflections

  3. Try the search and filters

    Use tags like "night owl," "chronic condition," or "urban lifestyle" to find relevant experiences

Understanding Our Approach to Organization

Why Our Tags and Categories Matter

How we organize information shapes what stories get told and what gets left out. This archive uses community language rather than medical or wellness industry categories.

Our Approach vs. Traditional Health Categories

Community Language (Our Archive)
"Night owl schedule"

Respects natural rhythms and work requirements

"Chronic condition navigation"

Focuses on skills and agency, not diagnosis

"Community care practices"

Recognizes mutual support as health strategy

"Cultural food adaptation"

Honors tradition while acknowledging change

Traditional Categories
"Sleep disorder"

Pathologizes normal variation

"Chronic disease management"

Emphasizes medical control over personal agency

"Social support"

Makes community sound like a medical intervention

"Dietary modification"

Ignores cultural meaning of food changes

How Participants Control Their Own Stories

Choose Their Own Tags

Instead of fitting into pre-existing categories, participants can create tags that actually describe their experience

Example: "Urban foraging knowledge" rather than just "nutrition education"

Decide What to Share

Some share detailed health data, others focus on community stories, others share audio reflections‚Äîall equally valuable

Example: One participant shares fitness tracker data, another shares grandmother's recipe modifications

Update Over Time

Health journeys aren't static‚Äîparticipants can add new content or update their perspectives

Example: Adding how community garden participation changed during different seasons

Contributing Your Own Story

Why Your Story Matters

This archive exists to document how people actually create health in their daily lives‚Äînot just the stories that make it into wellness magazines or medical studies. Your experiences, challenges, and strategies contribute to a more complete picture of 21st-century health practices.

"I never thought my night shift schedule could be valuable research data. Now I see how archives usually miss people like me." ‚Äî Current archive participant

How to Contribute (Your Choice of Format)

Audio Story

Record 10-20 minutes sharing your health journey, daily routines, or community practices

  • Use your phone's voice recorder
  • Find a quiet space
  • Speak as if talking to a friend
  • We provide suggested questions if helpful

Photo Documentation

Document your health practices through images‚Äîmeals, activities, spaces, community connections

  • 5-15 photos with brief captions
  • Show what health looks like in your life
  • Include community spaces if relevant
  • Respect others' privacy in shared spaces

Written Reflection

Write about your health experiences, challenges, discoveries, or community practices

  • 500-1500 words (flexible)
  • Share what feels important to you
  • Include specific examples
  • Writing support available if needed

Data + Stories

Share health data (fitness tracker, sleep, habits) along with your interpretation of what it means

  • Screenshots or exported data
  • Your analysis of patterns
  • What data tracking teaches you
  • Privacy controls available

Privacy and Control

Choose Your Level of Sharing

  • Public: Available to all archive visitors
  • Researchers only: Available to academic researchers with ethical approval
  • Community only: Available to other archive participants
  • Private with excerpts: Share selected quotes/images while keeping full story private

Update or Remove Anytime

Your archive contribution belongs to you. You can update your story, add new content, or request removal at any time.

No Medical Information Required

You choose what health information to share. No diagnoses, medical records, or personal health information required.
